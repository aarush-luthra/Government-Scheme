## What your project does (right now)

### 1️⃣ It stores government scheme information in a smart way

You have written down information about some government schemes (like PM-KISAN, Ayushman Bharat, PMAY) in a structured format.

Your project:

* Takes this information
* Breaks it into small pieces
* Saves it in a way that a computer can *understand the meaning*, not just the words

---

### 2️⃣ It builds a “smart library” of schemes

Instead of keeping schemes as plain text files, your project:

* Converts scheme text into **embeddings** (numerical meaning)
* Stores them in a **vector database (ChromaDB)**

Think of it like:

> A library where books are organized by meaning, not by title.

---

### 3️⃣ It prepares the system to answer questions later

Right now, your project:

* Does **not yet talk to users**
* Does **not yet decide eligibility**

But it **prepares all the background work** so that later you can ask questions like:

* “Which schemes help farmers?”
* “What documents are needed for PMAY?”

And the system will already know **where to look**.

---

### 4️⃣ It avoids wrong or fake information

Your project:

* Does **not scrape websites**
* Does **not guess answers**
* Uses **fixed, verified scheme data**

This makes it:

* More reliable
* Easier to explain
* Safer for real users

---

## What it does NOT do yet

❌ It does not yet chat with users
❌ It does not yet check eligibility
❌ It does not yet give step-by-step advice automatically

Those are **next steps**, not missing parts.
